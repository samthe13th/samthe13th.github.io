The physics of asteroid impacts and exploding toasters

For a long time most geologists supported the theory that moon craters were actually calderas – the remnants of massive volcanos that had erupted and collapsed in on themselves. This would explain the often very uniform shape of moon craters. The competing theory at the time, was that the craters were caused by asteroid impacts, but this was often dismissed due to the fact that an impact from an object coming at an angle seemed unlikely to create a circular crater.

The reason craters are circular has to do with the way that the kinetic energy of the asteroid is being transformed. An asteroid that strikes a moon or a planet like our own at high speed like 30,000 m per second has a massive amount of kinetic energy associated with it that is rapidly converted to thermal energy on impact.
Kinetic Energy --> Thermal Energy
So what happens when the energy of an object is converted to thermal energy, aka HEAT, very quickly? It tends to explode. Or more specifically, it changes state to a gas very very quickly, which in turn expands, applying massive amount of pressure to everything in close proximity.

What do you always need for any explosion?

  • Rapid transformation of some type of energy into heat
  • Nuclear energy
  • Chemical energy
  • Kinetic energy
  • Oxygen


While most bombs rely on transforming the energy from chemical reactions into thermal energy, the reality is that that ANY type of energy that can be rapidly transformed into heat can cause an explosion, including kinetic energy.

One definition of HEAT or THERMAL ENERGY is "A measure of the kinetic energy of individual molecules in an object".

In the case of a solid object like an asteroid, the motion of individual molecules is small in comparison to the speed of the object as a whole, and the energy of these molecules is smaller then the energy holding the asteroid together. During a collison though, the energy associated with the direction motion of the asteriod is converted into the motion of individual molecules.

Remember, the conservation of energy tells us that the energy of a closed system is conserved, so the kinetic energy of an asteroid MUST get converted to another form of energy.



If the thermal energy resulting from imapct is greater then the energy holding the asteroid's molecules together as a solid object, then the object asteroid changes state, and it's molecules speed off in every direciton as a gas. THIS is why high speed asteroids mostly vaporize upon impact, resulting in a explosive shockwave.

The Earth has several examples of high-speed asteroid impact craters, the largest of which is believed to be from an asteroid that wiped out the dinosaurs. The blast caused by this asteroid was the equivalent of over a billion nuclear bombs!
